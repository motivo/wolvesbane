Wolvesbane New Player Guide

Version 2
A calmer overview of the world: city, road, ruins, and dungeon. It fits the guide well because Wolvesbane progression is really about moving from safety into danger in the right order.

If your goal on Wolvesbane is to reach hard bosses and real endgame farming, stop thinking in terms of broad “what is this shard about?” advice. The useful question is simpler: what should I do next?

This version is written to answer that directly. It is not a lore page. It is a practical route for a new player who wants to stop being weak, start making money, and grow into pet-based boss farming as fast as possible.

The Core Decision

If your destination is the hardest content, start on the pet path.

You can play melee, mage, crafter, or some hybrid for fun. But if you want the cleanest road into serious farming, champs, and bosses, the strongest pattern from the Discord is still pets. Balrons, champion pressure, token farming, and high-end efficiency all keep pointing in the same direction.

Step 1: Get The Client Working Correctly

Use the Wolvesbane custom client first.

  • Website: http://wolvesbaneuo.com
  • Host: WolvesbaneUO.ddns.net
  • Port: 2593
  • Launcher: http://wolvesbaneuo.ddns.net/downloads/WolvesbaneUO.exe

Players repeatedly pointed out that Wolvesbane has custom map content. If you try to use a normal client with Razor pointed at it, you can get broken or missing content.

Do this in order:

  1. Download the official Wolvesbane launcher.
  2. Run it and let it patch fully.
  3. Only after that, move into ClassicUO, Razor, or Razor Enhanced if you want a better setup.
  4. If you use Razor Enhanced, update RE separately if needed. Several players said the launcher can leave RE on an older version.

Step 2: Pick A Focused Start

The starting packages mentioned by players were:

  • 3 skills at 120
  • 7 skills at 100
  • all skills at 45

If you care about progression speed, take the focused route, not the broad one. One experienced player explicitly preferred 3 x 120.

Wolvesbane also appears to use a stat-ball system. One shared example used:

  • 100 STR
  • 175 DEX
  • 25 INT

The exact spread is less important than the rule: do not make random choices at the start.

A confirmed practical start for the pet path is Animal Taming 120 and Animal Lore 120. The third skill is still flexible, but this core is enough to run the druids for an Instant Taming Deed, and once you have that deed you can tame a rainbow pet quickly.

Step 3: Do These Things In Order

  1. Go straight to the training area.
  2. Take the training weapons from the tables.
  3. Grab the newbie armor from the trainer area.
  4. Set up your loot bag before any real farming.
  5. Train just enough to stop feeling weak.
  6. Go to the new player water dungeon and start farming gold and tokens.

That is the real early game. If you are still asking yourself what to do next in the first few hours, the answer is usually one of these: train, fix loot, or farm the water dungeon.

Step 4: Fix Utility Before You Chase Gear

The best early utility advice in the Discord was not “buy a great weapon.” It was “buy the tools that remove friction.”

Players reported that Wolvesbane has a stone selling:

  • Rogue spellbook
  • Avatar spellbook

Reported price:

  • 25,000 gold

That price is still current.

Why these matter:

  • The Rogue book reportedly includes Intimidation, which gives +50 to melee weapon skills.
  • The Avatar book lets you use Mark and Gate through chivalry instead of magery.

If they are still available around that price, buy them early. They solve more problems than most random gear upgrades.

Important warning: turn Intimidation off while training. Players specifically said it may interfere with skill gain.

Also vote early. Players directly called out the vote stone as a source of useful utility, including a Bag of Holding.

Step 5: Learn Tokens Properly

Tokens are one of the core progression currencies on Wolvesbane. If you ignore them early, you slow your entire character down.

Set up your loot bag so [loot works cleanly. Your goal is simple:

  • clear corpses fast
  • collect tokens consistently
  • avoid flooding your backpack with junk

Players reported that Lady Luck exchanges tokens for gold, and the most common benchmark repeated was:

  • 4,000 tokens = 1,000,000 gold

That rate is still current, so treat 4,000 tokens as your first obvious conversion checkpoint. That is the point where a lot of players start feeling the shard open up.

Quality-of-life trick: players kept repeating the 4,000-token chunk because it converts cleanly. There are also player-made scripts and macros for cashing in tokens and banking the checks once you start farming in bulk.

Step 6: Know When You Are Ready To Leave The Beginner Phase

Do not leave the “new player” phase just because you are bored. Leave it when these things are true:

  • You can survive without constant panic.
  • Your loot setup works.
  • You know how to turn tokens into gold.
  • You have bought your first useful utility items.
  • You are no longer depending on random luck to make progress.

Once those are true, then champs and serious pet progression start to make sense.

Step 7: Start Building A Real Pet Farming Loop

Your first serious goal is not a perfect finished build. It is becoming functional enough with pets that you can farm safely and consistently.

The clearest example in Discord was balrons:

  • rottweilers were repeatedly recommended against balrons
  • balrons were said to drop 25k-50k tokens when looted
  • players warned that balrons may cast Blood Oath

That tells you how Wolvesbane works at the high end:

  • hard content can pay very well
  • pets are often the safer and more efficient way to farm it
  • trying to brute-force bosses in melee because it feels cool is a good way to die for no reason

If you want a real milestone to aim at, use this one: once you can kill balrons safely with pets and reliably collect the token payout, you are no longer stuck in the weak early game.

Step 8: Learn Champs The Right Way

Champion content matters on Wolvesbane. It is part of normal progression.

Useful specifics from Discord:

  • Baracoon was recommended because it is less spread out.
  • Ilshenar sleeping dragon was suggested as a place to gain valor.

The biggest reward rule to remember is this: players said powerscrolls only drop from champion spawns on the Felucca facet. Other champion content may still give valor, artifacts, scrolls of transcendence, and gold.

Also, if you are doing champs with pets, tag the boss yourself. FAQ discussion called this out directly: if your character never hits the boss, you may miss the reward. If rewards still do not appear, check weight and item count before assuming the spawn is broken.

Step 9: Donation Or Grind

Wolvesbane does not read like “donate or you cannot compete.” It reads more like “donate if you want to remove friction.”

The donation items that look most useful to a new player are:

  • Bag of Holding
  • Tamers Little Helper
  • Everlasting Bandages
  • Public Gate Book
  • Master Keys
  • Pet Max Level Deed
  • Power Scroll Reward Deed
  • Instant Taming Deed

The donation vendor still has these items at the same prices.

If you are donating, the sensible order is:

  1. Bag of Holding if inventory pain is slowing everything.
  2. Tamers Little Helper if you are fully committed to the pet path.
  3. Everlasting Bandages if healing friction is constant.
  4. Public Gate Book if travel waste is hurting your loop.
  5. Master Keys only after you have enough resources for them to matter.

If you are not donating, the practical replacement order is:

  1. Vote for utility.
  2. Buy the custom spellbooks early.
  3. Farm tokens and convert them regularly.
  4. Use champs and drops for progression instead of trying to buy comfort too early.

Important: several useful items also have non-donation routes. Players specifically mentioned the vote stone for Bag of Holding, tailoring for the Spellbook Bag, gold-stone access for at least some pet-related items, Level 4 chests for several deed types, and champion content for powerscroll progression.

Best Pet Path By Stage

Based on the accessible Discord discussions, this is the clearest pet power ladder I can justify right now.

  1. Early game: use the shard support systems first, not rare pets. Get through training area, newbie armor, loot setup, water dungeon, utility books, and token conversion before worrying about prestige pets.
  2. First real pet strength: the strongest repeated practical advice is rottweilers for balrons. If your goal is a working power checkpoint, balron farming with rottweilers is the clearest one the Discord gives us.
  3. Midgame power systems: start caring about Pet Max Level Deeds, Pet AI Deeds, and specialized control gear once your base farming loop works.
  4. Longer-term pet systems: move into Evo Wolf and other evo-pet progression once you already have a stable character, because evo pets look like a deep investment path, not a first-day shortcut.
  5. Very late progression: bossing, post-120 skill growth, and shard-specific pet optimization start overlapping. That is where things like high PS, control gear, AI choices, and max-level pet investment seem to matter most.

The one point I can state cleanly is this: if you are still weak, do not chase “best pet in theory.” Build the route that gets you to a safe balron farm first.

Useful specifics from the archive:

  • The dog line is considered strong, and one player specifically said the dogs are powerful and not aggressive when taming. The named list included Great Dane, Newfoundland, St. Bernard, Black Russian Terrier, Malamute, and Rottweiler.
  • Newfoundland dogs were called “absolute beast at fighting” and one player said they can help clear a champ with no problems.
  • Pandas were described by one veteran as better than rottweilers for balrons because balrons are weakest to cold, which is the panda’s main damage. The same player also said mercs are better for champs.
  • Several later players said breeding pandas is not worth it because babies lose overcapped damage or other advantages.
  • Wolf Spider keeps showing up as a meaningful strong pet in practical discussion.
  • One archived owner comment from 2020 said “The Spider is in my opinion the best pet available,” while also noting it is hard to control.
  • Another old shard note says Wolf Spider requires about 145.1 taming and lore, which explains why it feels awkward for underbuilt tamers.
  • One older FAQ message said that with 120 Taming and 120 Lore, rainbow pets are fairly easy to get.
  • One older rainbow discussion claimed the rainbow pet can rez you even when it is not alive. Treat that as a shard-specific old FAQ claim worth remembering, but still worth confirming in live play.
  • Griffins also show up as a real option. Archived discussion says they can roll with strong overcapped stats, but they are not the clean beginner path and can have awkward follower-slot behavior.
  • There is also late-game player enthusiasm around higher-generation cats, but that reads more like a long-term project than a first efficient route.

Mercenary Path

Mercenaries are a real power system on Wolvesbane, but the accessible Discord messages do not yet give a full beginner merc build guide. What is confirmed is still useful:

  • Guardian Merc can drop the merc contract/deed. A player called it a mean boss with a low but real drop chance.
  • Mercenary commands exist, though one player reported client crashes around merc help and [getpet.
  • A merc stat and skill gump exists.
  • Merc-related training clearly exists, and the owner said merc weapon skills train “just like a pet” or through normal fighting / training wolves.
  • Everlasting Bandages can be given to a merc.
  • Some “true artifacts” were explicitly said to affect merc stats.
  • One archived FAQ answer said for champs you want a merc, while for balrons that same veteran preferred pandas.
  • Another archived FAQ note said a merc can wear Evo armor and use Bloody Katana, Axe of Kronik, and Lucifers Bow, and can reach Avenger at stage 5 at 15 mil ep.
  • Archived player trade posts show people selling fully leveled mercs trained to Avenger and Defender, which suggests those paths are considered meaningful end states.
  • Archived player discussion also says skill mounts apply their bonuses to mercs when mounted.

Practical conclusion: mercs are real and farmable, and there is one strong opinion in the archive that for champs you want a merc. The tamer route is still better documented overall, but the archive now shows mercs are not secondary fluff.

Level Deeds, Evo Training, And How They Actually Work

The archive finally gave us the missing mechanical explanation, not just the item names.

  • A Level Item Deed makes an item gain EXP from kills, not hits.
  • After applying it, left click the item and use Information to spend its points.
  • There is also a boss that drops +5, +10, +15, and +20 level deeds for weapons or armor that already have the base Level Item Deed applied.
  • Evo pets, weapons, and armor can be trained on the North Side Training Wolves.
  • Players also explicitly said the Vorpal Bunnies are used to raise pet levels and item levels after the deed is applied.
  • One archived tip said it is better to max the pet on the training wolves first, then add max-level deeds and move to the bunnies.
  • For evo pets, one old FAQ answer says you can check them with [ep.

Weapon-specific archived advice is also stronger now:

  • One player said they would start with a runic evo weapon, fully evolve it, then apply the level deed and level it further on the bunnies.
  • The same discussion also says some regular weapons with a level deed can end up stronger than an evo weapon with a level deed on it, so the best final answer may depend on the base weapon and your build.
  • A veteran specifically said spending a one-handed deed and level deed on an exceptional halberd was a bad idea, which is one of the clearest warnings we have against wasting rare upgrade items.

Pet AI Deeds: The Most Useful Archived Advice

  • A pet can only have one AI deed active at a time.
  • If you apply a new AI deed, it starts from the beginning again.
  • One archived player recommendation rated Spell Weaving and Mysticism highest for non-merc pets because they give area damage and healing.
  • The same player rated Archery very highly for mercs if the merc is intended to use a bow and stand off.
  • Another later player said dungeon treasure chests are a source for pet AI spell deeds.

This is one of the clearer examples of the archive turning vague “pet progression” into actual shard advice.

Useful Tips And Tricks

  • Use the Wolvesbane custom client even if you prefer Razor.
  • If you use CUO and Razor Enhanced, update RE separately when needed.
  • In the CUO setup discussion, players specifically called out Ctrl + mouse wheel zoom, always-daytime mode, larger bags and containers, and drag-select health bars as useful comfort settings.
  • If you are runecrafting, bring the item back to full durability before every meaningful craft step.
  • Use BODs to get POF.
  • Armor is usually the cheaper runecrafting target than weapons.
  • Hit chance increase was discussed as having a 45 percent cap.
  • Shrunken pets may change ID each time, so pet macros built around a stale serial can fail.
  • Chaga Mushrooms were said to spawn near the entrance to the abyssal infernal champion area, and another player said the spider dungeon boss spawns many of them.
  • If you are working toward levelable gear, remember: item level EXP comes from kills, not hits.
  • If you are looking for powerful but tameable pets without a donation shortcut, archived player talk suggests the dog line, wolf spider, and panda are all worth understanding early.

What To Avoid

  • Do not spread your starting choices too thin.
  • Do not buy comfort before your farming loop works.
  • Do not let pets do 100 percent of a champ boss kill if you care about rewards.
  • Do not runecraft valuable gear casually.
  • Do not delay utility purchases while chasing random upgrades.
